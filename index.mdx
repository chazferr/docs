Public markets distribute information uniformly. Every participant on every platform receives the same price feeds, the same volume rankings, the same aggregated signals processed through the same interfaces at the same time. This is not a flaw in market design. It is a structural feature. Public information, by definition, belongs to no one.

The consequence for traders is significant. When discovery is homogenized, timing advantages compress toward zero. When every participant sees the same opportunity at the same moment, the opportunity is no longer an opportunity. It is consensus. And consensus, in liquid markets, is already priced.

The question is not whether more information exists. It does. The question is whether any of it is relevant to how a specific trader actually operates. How they size positions. Which risk profiles they engage with. Where they hesitate. What they consistently ignore. This behavioral layer is invisible to every platform that only records what a trader executed.

Omnera captures what happens before execution. Every session builds a behavioral model unique to each user. The feed does not show what is trending. It shows what is relevant. Across spot, perpetual futures, and prediction markets. Across any supported chain. In a single interface.

The market is public. Perspective is not.
