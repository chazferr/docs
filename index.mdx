Every trading platform distributes the same information to every user at the same time. The same price feeds, volume rankings, and trending lists, all generated from the same aggregate data. This is not a failure of design. It is a constraint of architecture.

When a million traders see the same signal at the same moment, the signal contains no differentiation. The opportunity visible on every screen simultaneously is, by the time it appears, consensus. Markets price consensus instantly.

Omnera breaks this symmetry. The feed ranks opportunities by relevance to each individual user, derived from a behavioral model that updates every session. The model captures what other platforms cannot: not just what a trader executes, but what they consider, how long they consider it, and what they pass on.

Inputs to the model include scroll depth, dwell time on specific opportunities, near-executions that did not complete, and instrument types revisited across sessions. These signals are invisible to any system that only records transactions. They are the difference between knowing what someone did and understanding how they think.

The feed operates across spot, perpetual futures, and prediction markets on Solana and Base. A trader on session thirty receives a fundamentally different feed than a trader on session one. The model compounds with use. Every session that passes without a profile is a session where discovery is identical to every other participant on the market.

The market is public. Perspective is not.
