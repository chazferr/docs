## The Problem

You think you have a strategy. Maybe it's:
- "Buy tokens under $100K market cap with locked liquidity"
- "Enter when holder count crosses 500"
- "Sell at 2x, cut losses at 50%"

But does it actually work? You won't know until you've lost real money finding out.

## The Solution

Run your exact filters and rules against **real Solana data** from the last 7-30 days. Not cleaned-up data. Not survivorship-biased data. Real data, with realistic slippage, failed tokens included, and all the chaos of actual markets.

<Info>
  Most backtesting tools quietly remove failed tokens from their data. Omnera includes them, because you would have traded them too.
</Info>

## What Gets Tested

### Your Exact Rules

Define your strategy however you trade:

```
Entry Conditions:
- Market cap between $50K and $500K
- Liquidity locked for 30+ days
- Top 10 holders own less than 40%
- At least 100 holders
- Creator wallet has 2+ previous tokens

Exit Conditions:
- Take profit at 2x
- Stop loss at -40%
- Time-based exit after 4 hours if flat
```

### Against Real Conditions

| What's Included | Why It Matters |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Realistic slippage | Your backtested entry price matches what you'd actually get |
| Failed transactions | Some trades wouldn't have executed. We account for that |
| Failed tokens | Tokens that went to zero are in the data |
| Gas costs | Priority fees during congestion are included |
| Timing | Execution delays based on actual network conditions |

## Understanding Your Results

### Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Means | What's Good |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Win Rate | % of profitable trades | Depends on R:R, but >35% with good sizing |
| Average R | Avg win รท avg loss | Above 2.0 means winners outpace losers |
| Max Drawdown | Worst peak-to-trough decline | Under 30% for most traders |
| Max Losing Streak | Most consecutive losses | Can you emotionally handle this? |
| Expectancy | Average $ per trade | Positive = you have edge |

## No Survivorship Bias

<Warning>
  Most backtesting data quietly removes failed tokens. This makes strategies look better than they are. Omnera includes all tokens, including those that went to zero, because when you're trading live, you don't get to skip them.
</Warning>

When a token failed during your backtest period, it's in there. When liquidity was pulled, it's in there. The results you see are the results you would have actually gotten.
