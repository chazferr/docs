A prediction market is a venue where participants take positions on the outcome of verifiable real-world events. Contract prices reflect the aggregate probability the market assigns to each outcome. A contract priced at 0.74 implies approximately 74 percent probability. Contracts settle to binary outcomes: affirmative resolution at 1, negative at 0. Academic research has consistently shown that prediction market prices aggregate dispersed information with accuracy comparable to expert consensus.

Prediction markets reveal a dimension of trader behavior that spot and futures do not. Engagement with event-driven contracts signals how you process uncertainty, time horizon, and conviction. A trader who enters only when implied probability drops below a threshold is revealing a contrarian pattern. A trader who favors short-duration contracts is revealing time horizon preferences. A trader who avoids prediction markets entirely while actively trading spot is revealing instrument-type boundaries. These behavioral inputs do not appear in spot or futures activity.

Omnera surfaces prediction market contracts within the same feed as spot and perpetual futures, ranked by individual relevance. If your behavioral profile reflects interest in event-driven markets, relevant contracts appear without a separate interface. Each prediction market interaction refines the model in ways that other instruments cannot replicate. A platform without this data sees an incomplete version of how you think.

Prediction market features are available where legally permitted. Users are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable local laws and regulations before participating. This is not financial advice.
