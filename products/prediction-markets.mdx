A prediction market is a venue where participants take positions on the outcome of verifiable real-world events. Contract prices reflect the aggregate probability the market assigns to each outcome. A contract priced at 0.74 implies approximately 74 percent probability of the associated outcome occurring.

Prediction markets reveal a dimension of trader behavior that spot and futures markets do not. A platform limited to spot and futures data sees an incomplete version of how a trader thinks.

## How Prediction Markets Work

**Price as probability**

The price of a binary prediction market contract functions as an implied probability.

P(event) = contract price / $1.00

This relationship holds under conditions of sufficient liquidity and diverse participant composition. Academic research has consistently demonstrated that prediction market prices aggregate dispersed information with accuracy comparable to expert consensus.

**Settlement**

- Contracts settle to binary outcomes upon event resolution
- Affirmative resolution settles at 1. Negative resolution settles at 0
- Settlement is determined by the underlying venue based on verifiable data sources

## Why This Matters for Behavioral Modeling

Engagement with event-driven contracts signals how a trader processes uncertainty, time horizon, and conviction. These are high-value behavioral inputs that do not appear in spot or futures activity.

- A trader who monitors prediction markets but only enters when implied probability drops below a threshold is revealing a contrarian pattern
- A trader who engages with short-duration event contracts is revealing time horizon preferences
- A trader who avoids prediction markets entirely while actively trading spot and futures is revealing instrument-type boundaries
- These signals fill a structural gap in the behavioral model that cannot be inferred from other instrument types
