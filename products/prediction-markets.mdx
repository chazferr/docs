A prediction market is a venue where participants take positions on the outcome of verifiable real-world events. Contract prices reflect the aggregate probability the market assigns to each outcome at a given point in time. A contract priced at 0.74 implies the market assigns approximately 74 percent probability to the associated outcome occurring.

## Price as Probability

The price of a binary prediction market contract can be interpreted as an implied probability under the assumption of risk-neutral pricing. For a binary contract with outcomes 1 and 0:

P(event) ≈ contract price / $1.00

This relationship holds when markets are sufficiently liquid and participant composition is diverse. Prediction markets are well-studied as mechanisms for aggregating dispersed information. Under appropriate conditions, market prices have demonstrated forecasting accuracy comparable to expert consensus.

## Integration with the Feed

Prediction market opportunities appear within the same feed as spot and perpetual futures positions. A user whose behavioral profile reflects engagement with event-driven markets will see relevant contracts matched to their profile.

Positions are taken through the same execution interface used across all instrument types. No separate application is required.

## Settlement

Prediction market contracts settle to binary outcomes upon event resolution. A contract resolving affirmatively settles at 1. A contract resolving negatively settles at 0. Settlement is determined by the underlying venue based on verifiable real-world data sources.
