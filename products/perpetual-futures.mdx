A perpetual futures contract provides leveraged exposure to a crypto asset without a fixed settlement date. Unlike dated futures, which expire and require rollover, perpetual contracts remain open indefinitely. This makes them the dominant instrument for directional exposure in crypto markets, where the majority of trading volume on centralized venues occurs in perpetuals rather than spot.

## Funding Rate Mechanism

Perpetual contracts maintain price alignment with the underlying spot market through a periodic funding rate exchanged between counterparties. When the perpetual trades at a premium to spot, long positions pay short positions. When it trades at a discount, short positions pay long positions. This mechanism anchors the perpetual price to spot without requiring physical settlement.

The funding rate r at interval t is typically expressed as:

r(t) = clamp((P_perp - P_spot) / P_spot, -cap, +cap)

where P_perp is the mark price of the perpetual contract, P_spot is the reference spot price, and cap is the maximum funding rate permitted per interval by the venue.

## Margin and Liquidation

Positions require initial margin to open and maintenance margin to remain open. If the mark price moves against a position such that remaining margin falls below the maintenance threshold, the position is subject to liquidation. Liquidation mechanics are determined by the underlying venue and may include partial liquidation and insurance fund participation.
