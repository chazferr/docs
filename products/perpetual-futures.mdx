A perpetual futures contract provides leveraged exposure to a crypto asset without a fixed settlement date. Unlike dated futures, perpetual contracts remain open indefinitely, making them the dominant instrument for directional exposure in crypto markets. Most platforms present perpetuals in isolation. You navigate to a separate section and manually assess whether the funding environment and basis spread justify the position. The decision is fragmented before it begins.

Perpetual contracts maintain price alignment with spot through a periodic funding rate exchanged between counterparties. When the perpetual trades at a premium, longs pay shorts. When it trades at a discount, shorts pay longs. The rate at interval t is r(t) = clamp((P_perp - P_spot) / P_spot, -cap, +cap), where P_perp is the mark price, P_spot is the spot reference, and cap is the venue's maximum rate per interval. Positions require margin to open and maintain. If remaining margin falls below the maintenance threshold, the position is liquidated.
