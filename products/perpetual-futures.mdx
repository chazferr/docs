A perpetual futures contract provides leveraged exposure to a crypto asset without a fixed settlement date. Unlike dated futures, perpetual contracts remain open indefinitely. This makes them the dominant instrument for directional exposure in crypto markets, where the majority of trading volume occurs in perpetuals rather than spot.

Most platforms present perpetual futures in isolation. A trader must navigate to a separate section, then manually assess whether the funding environment and basis spread justify the position. This separation fragments the decision process.

## How Perpetual Futures Work

**Funding rate mechanism**

Perpetual contracts maintain alignment with the underlying spot price through a periodic funding rate exchanged between counterparties.

- When the perpetual trades at a premium to spot, long positions pay short positions
- When it trades at a discount, short positions pay long positions
- This mechanism anchors the perpetual price to spot without requiring physical settlement

The funding rate r at interval t is typically expressed as:

r(t) = clamp((P_perp - P_spot) / P_spot, -cap, +cap)

where P_perp is the mark price of the perpetual, P_spot is the reference spot price, and cap is the maximum funding rate permitted per interval by the venue.

**Margin and liquidation**

- Positions require initial margin to open and maintenance margin to remain open
- If the mark price moves against a position such that remaining margin falls below the maintenance threshold, the position is subject to liquidation
- Liquidation mechanics are determined by the underlying venue and may include partial liquidation and insurance fund participation
