The standard sequence for evaluating an on-chain opportunity involves opening multiple tools, cross-referencing data manually, and synthesizing information from sources that were not designed to work together. By the time the evaluation is complete, the opportunity has either moved or the trader has entered on incomplete information. The bottleneck is not analysis skill. It is the time cost of assembling context from fragmented sources.

Omnera eliminates this assembly step. When a user engages with an opportunity in the feed, structured context surfaces automatically at the point of decision.

## What Surfaces

The signal layer presents three categories of information at the moment a user is evaluating an opportunity.

On-chain data includes wallet activity among the asset's largest holders, transaction patterns that diverge from the asset's baseline, and contract-level risk indicators including mint authority status, liquidity lock state, and holder concentration metrics.

Historical pattern data describes how the asset has behaved under comparable market conditions in prior periods. This includes volatility distribution, drawdown frequency, and recovery patterns across similar market regimes.

Behavioral context compares the current opportunity against the user's feed history. If the user has previously engaged with similar opportunities, the system surfaces how those prior opportunities performed after the user's point of engagement.

## What Does Not Surface

The signal layer does not generate trade recommendations. It does not predict future price movement. It does not assign buy or sell signals. Markets are non-stationary. Historical patterns are descriptive, not prescriptive.

## Timing

Context assembly occurs at the point of decision, not before and not after. The intelligence is not a research dashboard consulted separately. It surfaces inline, within the feed, at the moment the user's attention is on a specific opportunity. Every second spent switching between external tools is a second the market is moving.

## Disclaimer

Pre-trade intelligence is informational only. It does not constitute financial advice. Past market behavior does not guarantee future results. All trading decisions are made solely by the user.
