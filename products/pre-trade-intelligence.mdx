The standard sequence for evaluating an on-chain opportunity involves opening multiple tools, cross-referencing data manually, and synthesizing information from sources that were not designed to work together. By the time the evaluation is complete, the opportunity has repriced or the trader has entered on incomplete information.

The bottleneck is not analysis skill. It is the time cost of assembling context from fragmented sources.

## How It Works

When a user engages with an opportunity in the feed, structured context surfaces automatically at the point of decision. There is no separate research view. Intelligence appears inline, within the feed, at the moment the user's attention is on a specific opportunity.

Three categories of information surface:

**On-chain data**
- Wallet activity among the asset's largest holders
- Transaction patterns that diverge from the asset's baseline
- Contract-level risk indicators: mint authority status, liquidity lock state, holder concentration

**Historical patterns**
- How the asset has behaved under comparable market conditions in prior periods
- Volatility distribution, drawdown frequency, and recovery patterns across similar market regimes

**Behavioral context**
- How the current opportunity compares to the user's feed history
- If the user has previously engaged with similar opportunities, the system surfaces how those prior opportunities performed after the user's point of engagement

## What Does Not Surface

- No trade recommendations. No buy or sell signals
- No price predictions. Markets are non-stationary. Historical patterns are descriptive, not prescriptive
- No confidence scores or probability estimates on future price movement

The signal layer provides context. The user makes the decision.

## Why Timing Matters

Context assembled after the decision is a post-mortem. Context assembled minutes before the decision is research that may be stale by execution. Context assembled at the point of decision is the only timing that matches how markets move.

Every second spent switching between external tools is a second the market is repricing.

## Design Constraints

- Intelligence surfaces inline within the feed at the moment of evaluation
- No separate dashboard or research interface required
- On-chain, historical, and behavioral signals are pre-assembled. No manual cross-referencing
- The system is informational only. It does not execute on behalf of the user

## Disclaimer

Pre-trade intelligence is informational only. It does not constitute financial advice. Past market behavior does not guarantee future results. All trading decisions are made solely by the user.
