title: Cross-Chain Execution
description: One interface. Every supported chain. No bridges.
---

A trader holding SOL who identifies an opportunity on Base faces a sequence. Find a bridge. Wait for confirmation. Receive wrapped tokens. Swap into native assets. Execute. Each step introduces latency, cost, and failure risk. By the time the sequence completes, the opportunity has repriced or disappeared. This friction is structural. It filters out every cross-chain opportunity that requires speed.

Omnera removes the sequence entirely. You express intent. The system evaluates available paths across supported chains and selects the optimal route. Execution is atomic. The trade completes in full or does not execute at all. There is no partial state, no wrapped tokens, and no funds held in custody during transit. Native assets arrive on the destination chain.

This is not a bridge. Bridges custody assets during transfer, creating a window where funds are held by a third-party contract. That custody window has historically resulted in billions of dollars in losses across the industry. Atomic execution eliminates the custody window entirely. The trade settles or it reverts. There is no intermediate state where funds exist outside your control.

Solana and Base are live. Additional chains are in active development. As each chain is integrated, opportunities on that chain surface in the feed automatically. Each new chain also expands the behavioral model, because cross-chain activity reveals patterns that single-chain data cannot capture.

Execution should be invisible. Your job is to decide. Everything after the decision is infrastructure.
